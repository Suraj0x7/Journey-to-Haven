Culture and Tradition

Bihar has a rich cultural heritage, its the land of Buddhism and Jainism. Bihar is known as “The Land of Buddha” where Gautam Buddha attained enlightenment at Bodh Gaya. Bihar is a state of the culturally most mixed population. We have Hindu, Muslim, Jain, Buddhists, Sikhs, Christians living here. Bihar has a rich cultural background to be proud of it is associated with ‘Samudra Manthan’ of the Vedic era, Sita of Rama and Karna of Mahabharata. People are shy, honest, helping and confident of themselves.

Language

In Bihar major language spoken is Hindi, while Urdu and Santali are also spoken. Hindi is the official language of the state while Maithili, Bhojpuri, Angika, and Magahi are also widely spoken in the state.

Costumes

The traditional dress of Bihari people is dhoti-kurta for men and saree for women. The effect of western wear has effected a lot of people in Bihar and people wear western shirts and trousers. Western wear is getting popular not only among urban people but also among the rural male population. Salwar kameez if the main dress for women of urban Bihar and women wear saree in “Seedha Aanchal” style traditionally. Tussar Silk saree is the most used in Bihari costumes. Music and Dance

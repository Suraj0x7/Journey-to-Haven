Arunachal Pradesh, a veritable treasure house of nature, tucked away in the north eastern tip of India, invites you to relax in its picturesque hills and vales encircled on three sides by Bhutan, China and Myanmar. Come to Arunachal Pradesh to relive in nature in its full splendour. Enjoy the salubrious climate and meet its simple and hospitable people. For a tourist the options are varied, each more tempting than the other.



1. Summer


Summer season is considered pleasant in the upper hill destinations of Arunachal Pradesh but it tends to get quite warm in the lower ranges for example Itanagar. The temperature fluctuates between 20°c - 35°c during the summers. Despite the fact that, the journey may be quite tiresome and sweaty due to hot weather, being a hill destination there are many top tourist attractions and sightseeing places in Arunachal like Tawang, Roing, Ziro, Bomdila, Bhalukpong etc. These mentioned places give an appealing climate during summers; in fact, it acts as a summer retreat.
However, if you are the kind of traveller who prefer travelling light then summer season is the best. You can also visit multiple attractions like Ganga Lake, Itanagar Wildlife Sanctuary, Meghna Cave Temple, Ziro Puto, Pine Grove and Tipi. And the best part of it all is that, since summers are usually an off-season, you will get some great hotels options to stay in. You will definitely enjoy the fresh experience of the hills during summers in Arunachal Pradesh.


2. Winters


Temperature – Between 8° C and 14° C
Visiting Arunachal Pradesh during the winter is an ideal time since the weather is comfortable and it is the perfect season to go for sightseeing.
Winter: The best to visit Arunachal Pradesh
Due to its cold temperature, you get to enjoy and have a good time visiting many local attractions in Arunachal Pradesh. Winter is the best time to visit Arunachal Pradesh, especially if you plan on sightseeing and enjoying the winter festivals. Every year during the month of February, agricultural festivals namely Nyokum Yullo and Chalo Loku are celebrated. Another fun-filled festival is Pangsau Pass Winter Festival. It is joyously celebrated during the month of January and it is one of the most popular tourism events of Arunachal Pradesh.

Located in the upper Himalayan range, its elevation is really high giving it an alpine or a tundra type of weather. The temperature during winter remains quite chill but refreshing and it also fluctuates between 8°c and 14°c.

3. Monsoon


JTemperatures – Between 18° C and 29° C
Monsoon is the best time to cuddle up and gaze at the beautiful wonders outside. Right after the summer season, monsoon brings in cooling weather and gives some radiant to nature.
Although the monsoon lasts only for a few months, Arunachal Pradesh receives about 2000 to 4000 mm of rainfall. The weather keeps changing. Do not be surprised if you get to see darks clouds suddenly on a bright sunny day.
Visiting Arunachal during these months aren’t suggested. This is because of the reason that the chances of landslides are higher and you may end up getting held up in the middle of the road. However, if you are here during this season, do not miss to check-out the gorgeous waterfalls. Waterfalls in Arunachal Pradesh are the most beautiful during the monsoons.


Best time to visit Arunachal Pradesh


If you wish to enjoy your trip to Arunachal Pradesh to the most, The best time to visit Arunachal Pradesh is during winter. Winter falls between the months of October and March. This season brings out the beauty of snowy mountains, misty valleys and a beautiful carpet of virgin snow. Tawang also gives you a thrilling taste of winter sports on the snowy mountain slopes.
